<p><p>The hyperkalemia treatment market is divided into two main types: oral treatments and injectable therapies. Oral treatments typically include potassium binders that help reduce serum potassium levels through intestinal absorption. These are often preferred for outpatient management due to their ease of administration. In contrast, injectable treatments, such as calcium gluconate or insulin with glucose, are used in acute scenarios to rapidly lower potassium levels. Both routes aim to prevent serious complications associated with elevated potassium.</p></p>
